Physics of Cable Deployment

Field guide to the mechanics, mathematics, and operational realities governing this engine.

1. The Catenary & Parameter 'a'

A hanging cable under its own weight forms a Catenary, governed by the Catenary Parameter (a) — the curve's "stiffness".

a = H / q

H = Horizontal Tension · q = Cable weight per metre. High tension → flat curve. Low tension → baggy curve.

2. Two-Segment Model (Air vs. Water)

In shallow water the cable transitions from air to water. This engine calculates both segments separately using their respective weights and fuses them at the sea surface — eliminating "ghost" errors found in single-segment calculators.

3. Friction (Capstan Equation)

Laying (μ 0.3–0.5, dry): Chute grips cable, reduces tensioner load.
Recovery (μ 0.1–0.2, wet): Lubrication cuts winch torque demand.

4. Critical Zone (Hatched Red Area)

The CWD marks where air-segment weight dominates. Inside this zone: physics become unstable, MBR tightens rapidly, small vessel movements cause massive tension spikes.

5. Mathematical Formulary

y = a · [cosh(x / a) - 1]

Top Tension = √ ( H² + V² )

T_winch = T_sheave / e^(μ · θ)

CWD = 0.069H⁴ - 1.23H³ + 6.54H² + 2.9H + 3.15

Catenary Engine Reference

1. The Physics Model

This software models a "Composite Catenary". Unlike simple calculators, it accounts for the cable transitioning from water into air, maintaining physical continuity at the surface.

A. The "Chain" Equation

The fundamental shape is defined by the Catenary Parameter (a), which is the ratio of Horizontal Tension to Unit Weight.

a = H / w
y = a ¬∑ [ cosh( x / a ) - 1 ]
B. Forces at the Sheave (Top Tension)

Top Tension is the vector sum of the constant Horizontal Tension (H) and the accumulated Vertical Weight (Vtot) of the suspended line.

Vtot = (Lwater ¬∑ Wtwater) + (Lair ¬∑ Wtair)
Ttop = √( H2 + Vtot2 )

2. Geometry & Angles

Departure Angle (φ): Calculated relative to the Vertical axis (0° is straight down, 90° is horizontal).

φ = 90° - [ atan( Vtot / H ) ]

Air Gap Geometry: The length of cable in the air is not just a straight line; it includes the vertical drop to the waterline plus the wrap arc around the sheave radius.

Lair = (SheaveH - Radius) + (π ¬∑ Radius / 2)

3. The "Tri-Modal" Solver

This is a Reverse Engineering Engine using Newton-Raphson iteration.
